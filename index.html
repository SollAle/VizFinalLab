Worlds 2025 — Esports Meta & Performance

Project Motivation

Esports analytics plays a critical role in understanding competitive decision-making. This project explores how champion picks, bans, and team performance metrics at the 2025 League of Legends World Championship relate to match success.

Key Questions

  • Do highly picked or banned champions actually win more?
  • How does early-game advantage influence match outcomes?
  • Are certain regions consistently stronger across objective control and win rate?

What the Data Shows

The visualizations reveal that popularity does not always equal effectiveness. Some champions are frequently picked or banned despite mediocre win rates, while others quietly outperform expectations. At the team level, early-game gold advantage and objective control strongly correlate with winning, particularly among top-performing regions.

Dataset

Data sourced from Oracle’s Elixir, focusing on the Worlds 2025 Main Event. Champion-level and team-level statistics were cleaned, aggregated, and visualized using Python and Plotly.

Champion Meta Dashboard

Explore champion pick rate, ban rate, and win rate relationships across roles. Use hover interactions and legends to isolate specific roles or champions.

Team Performance Dashboard

Compare team success across regions using early-game advantage, aggression, and objective control metrics. Region filtering is built directly into the visualizations.
